# Idle City Logistics: Game Concepts & Mechanics

## 1. Core Currencies

These are global values displayed at the top of the HUD.

- **Credits ($)**: Generated by selling resources at the _Travel Market_ in the City. specific resource specific price.
- **Tech Points (TP)**: Generated by _Universities_ in the City. Used to unlock new buildings and efficiency upgrades.
- **Population (Pop)**: Generated by _Housing_. Required to operate buildings.
- **Influence (★)**: The Prestige Currency. Earned by "Selling the Company" (Reset).

## 2. Resource Tiers & The "Packing" Mechanic

The core loop is optimizing **Throughput**. Raw resources take up 1 Trunk Slot each. Refined/Packed goods take up 1 Slot but are worth 10x-100x more.

| Tier   | Item Name   | Created In        | Ingredients                      | Transport Efficiency               |
| :----- | :---------- | :---------------- | :------------------------------- | :--------------------------------- |
| **T0** | **Raw Log** | Forest: _Camp_    | None                             | Low (1 Slot = 1 Log)               |
| **T0** | **Wheat**   | Farm: _Field_     | None                             | Low (1 Slot = 1 Bushel)            |
| **T1** | **Plank**   | Forest: _Sawmill_ | 2 Logs                           | Medium (1 Slot = 5 Planks value)   |
| **T1** | **Flour**   | Farm: _Mill_      | 2 Wheat                          | Medium                             |
| **T2** | **Crate**   | Outpost: _Packer_ | 1 Plank + 10 Raw Item            | **High** (1 Slot = 10 Items + Box) |
| **T3** | **Ration**  | City: _Cannery_   | 1 Crate (Food) + 1 Crate (Steel) | **Ultra** (High Pop Stability)     |

## 3. The City (The Hub)

The City is where management happens. It consumes resources to grow.

### A. Infrastructure Buildings

- **Town Hall**: The HQ. Clicking it grants "Emergency Funding" ($). Displays stats.
- **Transport Depot**:
  - _Function_: Spawns the Truck entities.
  - _Upgrades_: "Fleet Size" (Max Trucks), "Engine Tech" (Speed).
- **Warehouse**:
  - _Function_: Increases global storage cap for resources.
  - _Mechanic_: If full, Trucks sit idle at the Depot (Efficiency loss).

### B. Population Buildings

- **Tenement / Apartment / Condo**:
  - _Function_: Periodically generates new People _if_ food is available.
  - _Cap_: Provides "Housing Space". Pop cannot exceed Housing Space.
- **Supermarket**:
  - _Function_: Automatically sells Food resources for Credits ($).
  - _Logic_: Player sets a "Sell Threshold" (e.g., keep 100 food for people, sell the rest).

### C. Specialist Buildings (Factories)

- **University**:
  - _Input_: Consumes Paper (from Wood) + Money.
  - _Output_: Tech Points.
- **Construction Yard**:
  - _Input_: Consumes Steel/Stone.
  - _Output_: Lowers building costs globally.

## 4. The Outposts (Resource Zones)

Each outpost (Forest, Mountain, Farm) has a dedicated **Loading Dock**.

### Buildings

1.  **Extractor** (e.g., Lumberjack Hut):
    - Must be placed next to a Tree Tile.
    - Produces `Raw Log` every `X` seconds.
2.  **Processor** (e.g., Sawmill):
    - Input: `Raw Log`. Output: `Plank`.
    - _Mechanic_: If placed adjacent to Extractor, transfer is instant. If far away, creates a "hauler" sprite (mini-transport).
3.  **Packer** (The Key Mechanic):
    - Input: `Packing Material` (Planks/Plastic) + `Product`.
    - Output: `Crated Product`.
    - _Why build it?_ Because your trucks have limited slots.

## 5. Transport Logic

The bottleneck is the road between City and Outpost.

**The Loop:**

1.  **Spawn**: Truck leaves City Depot.
2.  **Travel**: Moves along the "Highway" (Progress bar or visual road).
3.  **Arrival**: Arrives at Forest Dock.
4.  **Load**: Fills inventory slots.
    - _Priority System_: Player sets priority (e.g., "Load Crates first, then Planks, then Logs").
5.  **Return**: Travels back.
6.  **Unload**: Items added to Global City Inventory.

**Upgrades**:

- **Tarmac Roads**: Trucks move 20% faster.
- **Double Trailers**: Truck capacity 5 -> 10 slots.
- **Cold Chain Logistics**: Allows transport of Perishables without spoilage (if we add decay).

## 6. Population Mechanics

- **Growth**: Every 10 seconds, `Pop += Housing_Space * 0.01 * Happiness`.
- **Happiness**:
  - Starts at 100%.
  - -10% per missing food ration cycle.
  - -5% per Overcrowding (Pop > 90% Housing).
- **Consequence**:
  - Happiness < 50%: Research speed halves.
  - Happiness < 20%: Strikes (Total production freeze).

## 7. Prestige System: "Corporate Acquisition"

Reset the game to start a new City in a new region.

- **Trigger**: Reach $1 Billion Net Worth.
- **Reward**: **Stock Options** (Prestige Currency).
- **Spending Options**:
  - _Initial Investment_: Start next run with $1M.
  - _Nepotism_: Reduce Transport Upgrade costs by 10%.
  - _Nano-Tech_: Trucks carry +1 item per slot permanently.
